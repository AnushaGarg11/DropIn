Overview

An education loan is a financial tool designed to help students fund their education, covering tuition fees, living expenses, books, and other costs associated with their studies. These loans can be offered by both government and private financial institutions, with flexible repayment terms, typically starting after the completion of the course.

Top Education Loan Providers

  1. State Bank of India (SBI)
    • Interest Rate: Starts at 8.30%.
    • Repayment Terms: The repayment tenure is up to 15 years after course completion, with an additional 12-month moratorium period post-completion.
    • Eligibility Criteria: No collateral is required for loans up to INR 7.5 lakhs. Loans above this amount may require collateral. Processing fees are waived for loans up to INR 20 lakhs​( DNA India).

  2. HDFC Credila
    • Interest Rate: Varies between 9% and 13.80%, depending on the applicant's profile.
    • Repayment Terms: HDFC Credila offers a repayment tenure of 12-15 years, including the moratorium period, where only interest is payable during the course.
    • Loans up to INR 75 lakhs are available without collateral; beyond that, collateral may be required. Available for both domestic and international studies​( HiWiPay).

  3. Punjab National Bank (PNB)
    • Interest Rate: Typically starts around 7.30%, varying based on schemes.
    • Repayment Terms: Offers a repayment tenure of up to 15 years, with no collateral needed for loans up to INR 7.5 lakhs. Repayment begins one year after course completion.
    • Eligibility Criteria: Loans are available for students enrolled in recognized courses. PNB provides loans for both domestic and international studies​( DNA India).

  4. Axis Bank
    • Interest Rate: Ranges from 13.70% to 15.20%.
    • Repayment Terms: Axis Bank provides a 15-year repayment tenure, excluding the moratorium period (which covers course duration plus 6-12 months). There are no penalties for prepayment.
    • Eligibility Criteria: Loans up to INR 75 lakhs are available without collateral, and secured loans can be higher. Loans cover a wide range of institutions​( HiWiPay)​(DNA India).

What Documents Do You Need?

  1. Personal Documents:
    • Identity Proof (Aadhar card, PAN card, Voter ID, Passport, Driving License)
    • Address Proof (Aadhar card, Passport, Utility bills, Rental agreement, Voter ID)
    • Age Proof (Aadhar card, Birth certificate, Passport, Class 10/12 mark sheet)

  2. Educational Documents:
    • Admission Letter issued by the educational institution
    • Academic Records (Class 10/12 mark sheets, degree certificates, transcripts)
    • Fee Structure provided by the institution

  3. Financial Documents:
    • Income Proof of Co-Applicant/Guarantor (salary slips, Form 16, Income Tax Returns)
    • Bank Account Statement (latest 6-12 months of both applicant and co-applicant)
    • Assets and Liabilities Statement (if required)

  4. Collateral (if required):
    • Property Documents (title deed, property registration papers, valuation reports)
    • Insurance Policy assigned to the bank (if required)

  5. Other Documents:
    • Passport-sized Photographs of the applicant and co-applicant/guarantor
    • Declaration/Affidavit stating that funds will be used for educational purposes
